Ensemble Learning

Combining Multiple Models for Improved Flight Price Prediction

Understanding Ensemble Learning

Ensemble learning is a powerful machine learning paradigm that combines multiple models to obtain better predictive performance than could be achieved by any of the constituent models alone.

The fundamental principle behind ensemble methods is that a group of "weak learners" can come together to form a "strong learner." By combining multiple models, ensembles reduce the risk of selecting a poor model and often reduce overall variance and bias compared to single models.

In this project, we explore three powerful machine learning techniques and compare their performance:

  • Decision Trees: Simple, interpretable models that make decisions based on a series of questions
  • Random Forest: An ensemble method that builds multiple decision trees and combines their predictions
  • Support Vector Machines (SVM): Models that find the optimal hyperplane to separate different classes

By comparing these approaches, we can identify which techniques are most effective for predicting flight fare categories based on route characteristics, carrier information, and market dynamics.

Machine Learning Models in This Project

Decision Tree

A flowchart-like model that makes decisions based on asking a series of questions about the features.

  • Highly interpretable classification rules
  • Can handle both categorical and numerical data
  • Naturally handles multi-class problems
  • Requires minimal data preprocessing

Random Forest

A collection of decision trees where each tree is trained on a random subset of the data and features. The final prediction is made by aggregating results from all trees.

  • Reduces overfitting common in decision trees
  • Handles high-dimensional data well
  • Provides feature importance measurements
  • Robust against outliers and noise

Support Vector Machine

A powerful classifier that finds the optimal hyperplane to separate different classes with the maximum margin.

  • Effective in high dimensional spaces
  • Memory efficient as it uses only a subset of points
  • Versatile through different kernel functions
  • Works well with clear margin of separation

Key Findings

Our analysis reveals that distance categories and market share are consistently the most important predictors across all models. Routes with high market share tend to have more predictable pricing, while medium-distance routes show the most variability in fare categories. The ensemble approach of Random Forest provides the optimal balance between accuracy and interpretability for flight fare prediction.
