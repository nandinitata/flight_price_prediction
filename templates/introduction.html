SkySaver: US Flight Price Predictor

Analyzing flight data patterns and pricing strategies

The airline industry is a key part of the U.S. economy, helping millions of people travel every day. Airlines operate complex networks to connect cities, using both direct routes and hub-and-spoke systems. Ticket prices change constantly based on demand, competition, and seasonal trends. Some routes are highly competitive, leading to lower fares, while others remain stable due to less competition. Travelers often struggle to find the best time to book flights at the lowest price. Understanding how fares fluctuate can help passengers make smarter booking decisions. Airlines also adjust prices based on business trends, special events, and travel demand. Low-cost carriers have changed the market by offering cheaper alternatives to major airlines. Airports in busy cities face congestion, which can also impact ticket pricing and flight availability. With so many factors at play, analyzing flight pricing trends is essential for both airlines and travelers.

Many elements affect airfare, from fuel prices to weather disruptions and government regulations. Airlines use advanced pricing models to maximize profits while keeping seats filled. Some flights become more expensive closer to departure, while others drop in price at the last minute. Holiday seasons and major events create spikes in ticket demand, leading to higher fares. Travelers who understand these patterns can save money by booking at the right time. Competition between airlines is fierce, with carriers adjusting fares to attract more passengers. Regional airports sometimes offer lower fares than major hubs, giving travelers alternative options. Business travel trends and remote work have also changed demand for flights. As airfare pricing becomes more dynamic, having insights into market trends can be a game-changer. Understanding these factors helps passengers make informed choices and get the best deals on domestic flights.

Market Overview

The US domestic flight market represents an intricate network of routes connecting major metropolitan areas across the country. Our analysis examines diverse city pairs, investigating various aspects of air travel including pricing strategies, passenger volumes, and competitive dynamics. The comprehensive dataset spans multiple carriers, offering a complete perspective of the current market landscape and its evolving trends.

Exploratory Data Analysis

Distance vs Fare Relationship

Analysis of the relationship between flight distance and fare prices across different carriers.

  • Strong positive correlation between distance and fare prices
  • Higher price variability observed in long-haul routes (2000+ miles)
  • Major carriers (WN, UA, AA) demonstrate similar pricing strategies
  • Peak fares approximately $1000 for longest routes
  • Majority of fares concentrate in $200-400 range for medium distances

Fare Distribution Analysis

Distribution of fares across different carriers and routes.

  • Most fares concentrate between $150-350
  • WN (Southwest) shows highest frequency in lower fare ranges
  • Peak distribution around $200-250
  • Long tail extending to higher fares (>$500)
  • Different carriers show overlapping fare ranges

Fare Box Plot Analysis

Statistical distribution of fares by carrier showing quartiles and outliers.

  • AA, UA, DL demonstrate higher median fares with numerous outliers
  • Budget carriers (XP, MX, SY) consistently show lower median fares
  • G4 (Allegiant) maintains lowest median fare structure
  • Major carriers show significant outliers above $600
  • Typical fare range (IQR) falls between $150-300 across carriers

Carrier Market Share

Analysis of market share distribution among different carriers.

  • Competitive market with balanced distribution among carriers
  • G4 (Allegiant) leads with 9.82% market share
  • WN (Southwest) second at 8.85%, followed by XP at 8.74%
  • Most carriers maintain 6-8% market share range
  • No single carrier dominates the market

Route Category Analysis

Analysis of different route categories and their characteristics.

  • Medium and Long routes dominate with ~1.4M passengers each
  • Ultra-Long routes show lowest passenger volumes
  • Passenger preference trends toward medium-distance flights
  • Suggests optimal route length is in medium range
  • Clear distinction between short and long-haul market segments

Route Frequency Analysis

Analysis of route frequencies and popular flight paths.

  • Los Angeles has highest frequency (~250 flights)
  • Boston and New York show high activity (~150 flights each)
  • Houston, Dallas, and Chicago demonstrate moderate frequency (~50 flights)
  • Pattern reveals major US transportation hubs
  • High frequency routes align with business/tourism centers

Passenger Volume Analysis

Distribution of passenger volumes across different routes.

  • Los Angeles leads with ~180K passengers
  • New York follows with ~140K passengers
  • Miami shows strong volume (~100K passengers)
  • Atlanta and Chicago handle ~40-50K passengers
  • Las Vegas has the lowest volume among top routes (~30K passengers)
  • Reflects population centers and tourist destinations

Passenger Distribution Analysis

Detailed distribution analysis of passenger volumes.

  • Shows a right-skewed distribution
  • Most routes handle fewer than 1000 passengers
  • All carriers show similar patterns
  • Few routes with very high passenger volumes (>4000)
  • WN (Southwest) shows strong presence in high-volume routes
  • Major carriers (UA, AA, DL) have similar distributions

Fare Efficiency Analysis

Analysis of fare efficiency (cost per mile) across different routes.

  • Clear inverse relationship between distance and cost per mile
  • Short routes (<500 miles) have highest cost per mile (up to $3/mile)
  • Medium routes show moderate efficiency ($0.3-0.5/mile)
  • Long and Ultra-long routes most efficient (<$0.2/mile)
  • Shows economies of scale in longer flights
  • Some outliers in short routes with very high costs

Market Competition Analysis

Analysis of competition levels in different markets.

  • Miami leads with highest competition (12 carriers)
  • Los Angeles and Boston show strong competition (~11 carriers each)
  • Major hubs like Houston and New York maintain competitive markets (~10 carriers)
  • Smaller markets like Louisville have moderate competition (~7 carriers)
  • Competition levels correlate with airport size and market demand

Market Share vs Fare Analysis

Relationship between market share and fare pricing.

  • Higher market share (0.8-1.0) tends to have more pricing variation
  • Most fares cluster between $200-400 regardless of market share
  • Some carriers (UA, DL) show higher fares in routes where they dominate
  • Low market share (0.2-0.4) shows more consistent pricing
  • WN (Southwest) maintains relatively consistent fares across market shares

Seasonal Fare Analysis

Analysis of fare variations across different seasons.

  • Significant seasonal variation across quarters
  • Quarter 1 and 4 (winter months) show higher median fares
  • Legacy carriers (UA, AA, DL) consistently have higher fares
  • Budget carriers (G4, NK) maintain lower fares across quarters
  • Most extreme outliers appear in Q1 and Q2
  • All carriers show wider fare ranges in peak travel seasons
  • G4 (Allegiant) maintains the most consistent pricing across quarters
